<h2 aria-level="2" class="Body-headline" role="heading">Why choose Medicare Supplement?</h2>
<div class="Body-content">
<p>Because Original Medicare pays for only about 80% of your coverage.</p><p>Medicare Supplement (also known as Medigap) covers things that Original Medicare doesn’t, like copays, deductibles, and coinsurance (Need to know more about copays, deductibles and coinsurance? <a href="/medicare-solutions/medicare-basics">Learn more here</a>). It does all this with a steady, predictable, monthly bill you can budget for. And it can’t be cancelled. It will be renewed for as long as you pay your premium on time.</p>
</div>
</div>
</div>
</div>

<div class="Headline">
<h2>Is Medicare Supplement Insurance the right fit for you?</h2>
</div>
<div class="Flex-wrapper">
<div class="Flex-item">
<div class="Flex-innerWrap">
<img alt="check mark circle" class="Flex-innerItem" src="https://cdn.mutualofomaha.com/mutualofomaha/images/icons/utility-basics-check_circle-72.svg"/>
<span aria-level="3" class="u-h3 Flex-innerItem" role="heading">It's a great fit!</span>
</div>
<div>
<ul><li>You have or are planning to enroll in Original Medicare (Parts A and B).</li><li>You want more coverage than Part A and Part B give.</li><li>You’d rather pay a monthly, predictable bill than have to pay out of pocket for a large, unexpected medical bill.</li><li>You want to see any doctor who accepts Medicare — and you want to see them without a referral.</li><li>You like the flexibility and control of buying a separate dental and vision plan.</li><li>You want to be able to travel across the world with the confidence you will be covered.</li><li>You want the peace of mind that your insurance can’t ever be cancelled, as long as you don’t miss a premium.</li></ul>
</div>
</div>
<div class="Flex-item">
<div class="Flex-innerWrap">
<img alt="cancel x circle" class="Flex-innerItem" src="https://cdn.mutualofomaha.com/mutualofomaha/images/icons/utility-basics-x_circle-72.svg"/>
<span aria-level="3" class="u-h3 Flex-innerItem" role="heading">It's not the best solution for you.</span>
</div>
<div>
<ul><li>You are not planning on enrolling in Original Medicare (Parts A and B).</li><li>Your employer or your union help cover costs that Part A and Part B don’t cover.</li><li>You have Medicare Advantage (Part C) or are about to sign up for it.</li></ul>

<div>
<h2>When can I apply for Medicare Supplement Insurance?</h2>
<p><strong>Your open enrollment period starts when you turn 65</strong> or when you first sign up for Part B. It lasts for 6 months. This is the best time to sign up, because you’ll get the best possible rate and can’t be denied for a pre-existing condition. After this 6-month window closes, you can still apply but the insurance company may use medical underwriting.  There is also the possibility that you could be denied.</p>
